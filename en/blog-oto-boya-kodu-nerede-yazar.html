Where can you find a car paint code?

A simple guide to the usual label locations and why paint code verification matters before color matching.

Auto Paint2026-04-224 min read
Paint CodeAuto PaintColor Match

Why does the paint code matter?

A color name alone does not always guarantee the exact tone. Production year, batch, clear coat aging and sun exposure can change how the color appears.

Checking the original factory paint code gives the color matching process a safer starting point.

Common label locations

Paint codes are commonly found under the hood, on the driver door pillar, passenger door pillar, trunk area, engine bay label or service booklet.

The exact position can change by brand. If the label is unreadable, checking with VIN and vehicle details is safer.

Before application

The mixed paint should be tested on a small panel before the final application.

Metallic and pearl colors can be affected by gun pressure, coat count and clear coat thickness.

This article is for general information. Before application, technical data sheets, physical charts and expert checks should be considered.

Quick Notes

  • Physical verification is the safest method.
  • Technical data sheets should be checked before application.
  • Expert support reduces material and time loss.
