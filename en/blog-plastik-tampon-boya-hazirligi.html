How should plastic bumper parts be prepared before painting?

Cleaning, sanding and adhesion primer notes for bumpers and exterior plastic parts.

Application2026-04-224 min read
Bumper PaintPlastic PrimerPreparation

Cleaning comes first

Plastic parts can carry mold release agents, silicone, oil and road dirt. If these remain, paint adhesion becomes weak.

After cleaning, the part must dry completely before the next layer.

Sanding and primer

Plastic surfaces should be matted carefully without overly aggressive sanding.

A suitable plastic primer or adhesion promoter helps the paint layer bond more safely.

Flexibility and drying

Bumpers and similar parts flex, so the paint system must fit that need.

Shortening flash-off and drying times can cause swelling, orange peel or adhesion problems.

This article is for general information. Before application, technical data sheets, physical charts and expert checks should be considered.

Quick Notes

  • Physical verification is the safest method.
  • Technical data sheets should be checked before application.
  • Expert support reduces material and time loss.
