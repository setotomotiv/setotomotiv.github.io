What is the difference between RAL, NCS and Pantone?

A practical overview of common color systems used in paint, industry, design and surface selection.

Color Guide2026-04-225 min read
RALNCSPantone

When is RAL useful?

RAL is practical for industrial paint, metal, furniture, powder coating and general surface work.

Its simple code structure helps customers, suppliers and applicators communicate quickly.

When is NCS useful?

NCS describes color from a perception-based angle and can be useful for interior, design and architectural decisions.

Its code structure gives more detail about blackness, chromaticness and hue family.

When is Pantone useful?

Pantone is widely known in graphic design, packaging and brand identity work.

For paint production, a Pantone reference usually needs approximate matching, samples and physical chart verification.

This article is for general information. Before application, technical data sheets, physical charts and expert checks should be considered.

Quick Notes

  • Physical verification is the safest method.
  • Technical data sheets should be checked before application.
  • Expert support reduces material and time loss.
